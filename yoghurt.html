Yoghurt

Ingredients

8 cups milk (1/2 gallon) — whole or 2% are best, 1/2 cup commercial yogurt containing active cultures

Method

  1. 8 cups milk (1/2 gallon) — whole or 2% are best, but skim can also be used 1/2 cup commercial yogurt containing active cultures
  2. Stir the milk gently as it heats to make sure the bottom doesn't scorch and the milk doesn't boil over.
  3. Let the milk cool until it is just warm to the touch, 112°F to 115°F. Stir occasionally to prevent a skin from forming.
  4. Scoop out about a cup of warm milk into a bowl. Add the yogurt and whisk until smooth and the yogurt is dissolved in the milk.
  5. While whisking gently, pour the thinned yogurt into the warm milk. This inoculates the milk with the yogurt culture.
  6. Let the yogurt set for at least 4 hours or as long as overnight.
